The house

A house you disappear into the green of

Uma House sits low under the canopy in Suthep, reached by wooden walkways that thread through a dense, living garden. The rooms are teak and calm — deep blue walls, bamboo ceilings, carved headboards — the kind of quiet you feel in your shoulders.

There's a pool under the trees, a shared kitchen, and birds instead of traffic. It's a five-minute walk to Baan Kang Wat art village and the meditation temple at Wat Ram Poeng — which is why so many "one week" stays quietly become three.

The garden floor

A pool set into the greenery, loungers in the dapple, and a garden that does most of the talking. Cool water after a hot day of temples.

02

The walkways

Raised wooden decks carry you between the rooms through the trees — the reason people call it a treehouse. Slow down; the house wants you to.

03

The rooms

Blue teak rooms with air-conditioning, hot showers and real quiet. Clean, characterful, and cool even at midday under the canopy.
